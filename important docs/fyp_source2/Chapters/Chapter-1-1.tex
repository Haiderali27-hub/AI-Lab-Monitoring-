\chapter{Introduction}
\label{chap:introduction}

The growing dependence of higher education on digital infrastructure has transformed not only classroom delivery but also the way assessment is planned, conducted, and evaluated \cite{pmc_cbt}. In computer science and software engineering programs, practical laboratory examinations occupy a central place because they test problem solving, coding ability, system usage skills, and real-time decision-making in controlled academic settings. However, many institutions still conduct such examinations through fragmented workflows in which exam scheduling, laboratory readiness, invigilation, student attendance, suspicious-activity monitoring, submission collection, and post-exam reporting are handled using separate manual or semi-digital methods. This fragmentation reduces operational clarity and creates avoidable pressure on instructors and invigilators during already time-sensitive exam sessions. For this reason, the proposed AI-Driven Lab Exam Monitoring and Management System has been conceived as an integrated academic platform that improves visibility, control, traceability, and institutional reliability throughout the full life cycle of a laboratory examination.

The idea of combining examination management with monitoring support emerges from a practical institutional need \cite{mit_cbt}. In many university labs, supervisors are required to observe multiple students simultaneously, enforce timing rules, answer operational issues, verify candidate identity, track attendance, and later justify the validity of the conducted exam. When these responsibilities are distributed across paper lists, messaging groups, manual observation, local file collection, and after-the-fact reporting, the academic process becomes difficult to standardize. Errors are more likely to occur, evidence is harder to retain, and the fairness of the assessment may become difficult to demonstrate. A unified digital system can therefore provide an important institutional benefit by connecting planning, monitoring, secure execution, submission handling, and reporting into one auditable workflow \cite{integ1}.

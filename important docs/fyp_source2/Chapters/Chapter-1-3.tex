\section{Objectives}

The overall objective of the AI-Driven Lab Exam Monitoring and Management System is to design and implement an integrated platform that improves the planning, supervision, control, and post-exam accountability of practical laboratory examinations. This overall objective is operationalized through a set of specific objectives that guide both the software solution and the academic direction of the project.

The first objective is to digitize and centralize the administration of practical laboratory examinations so that scheduling, user management, laboratory allocation, and session configuration can be handled consistently from one platform. The second objective is to provide invigilators and instructors with a stronger real-time awareness of the ongoing exam through status monitoring, anomaly alerts, and organized session visibility \cite{pmc_cbt}. The third objective is to create a dependable submission and evidence-handling workflow so that academic decisions can later be supported through structured records instead of improvised documentation \cite{integ1}.

The fourth objective is to improve fairness and procedural reliability by enforcing controlled access, time-bound execution, and consistent handling of student activity in the lab environment \cite{rbac1}. The fifth objective is to reduce the burden of manual coordination placed on instructors and administrators by automating recurring operational tasks and by presenting consolidated reporting after exam completion. The sixth objective is to develop the project in a way that is academically meaningful, technically feasible within FYP constraints, and extensible for future institutional use.
